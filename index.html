Studio Recording

Studio Recording is all about having a precise acoustic environment in order to capture the best possible performance. There are many techniques and an extreme amount of subtle nuances that all come together to make a great recording. A lot of practices and techniques come down to personal preference and whatever best suits a particular sessions workflow. Certain applications require different hardware and even different software. But there are many tools and programs that are classified as "industry standards" or a "workhorse" piece of gear.

Neumann has been manufacturing microphones in Berlin Germany since 1928. The U87 microphone is the flagship of Neumann. You will find one of these microphones in every single professional studio you work in.

RCA stands for Recording Corporation of America. The RCA 44 was manufactured between the years 1930 and 1969. These rare vintage microphones are highly sought after because any signal that goes into instantly comes back choc full of harmonic distortion and lots of that vintage microphone "warmth"
